The problem

100 years ago, the first synthetic polymer micro plastic was created. This plastic was a revolutionary material that was able to withstand extreme amounts of pressure, but at the same time, it could be molded into practically anything. After 100 years, these plastics have been mass produced to a very large extent for almost every day to day appliance.

Every hour, 1.5 million pounds of plastic are dumped into the worlds oceans.

These Plastics Cause: 
  • Negative impact on the marine ecosystem
    • algal blooms
    • marine debris
    • seafood pollutants
    • CO2 emissions created by humans are absorbed into the ocean 
    • dead space in the ocean (garbage patches) 
    • death of thousands of marine animals
  • Carcinogens being released into the air
    • when plastics are burned, carcinogens and other pollutants are released into the air
  • Human Disease

The main question is why?

For hundreds of years humans have assumed that the vast oceans were limitless and prone to human impacts, and continue to treat them as our global garbage disposal. The world's oceans are polluted by millions of tons of plastic that are very harmful to ecosystems. In the modern era humans dump about eight million tons of plastic into the ocean every year according to National Geographic. 

Plastics don't degrade

The problem with all of this dumping is that plastics take 450 years to degrade and some studies even show some plastics takes about 1000 years to degrade. The plastic problem that humans have created negatively affects the oceans, ecosystems, and even humans. When plastics are ingested by marine life it causes infection, suffocation, and/or entanglement.

It is estimated that about 100 million marine animals die due to plastic pollution. The plastic in the ocean has also ended up in the food we eat because fish that eat the plastic can be caught easily, and be served on our plates unnoticed. It is currently estimated that by 2050 there will be a total of 937 million tons of plastic in the ocean. That is more than the estimated weight of fish in 2050. The world’s oceans ecosystems are endangered from single use plastics and this problem  will not disappear until a solution is developed.
