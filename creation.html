HOW WE DESIGNED OUR OWN BIOPLASTIC

Water

Water is a polar solvent which allows the various hydrocarbons to interact and form complex polymers. Also a vital part of the plastic degradation.

Starch

The starch provides long chains of hydrocarbons that provide strength and bonding regions for the other reactants.

Glycerin

The glycerin is the plasticizer, when bonded to the polymer chain it adds 3 alcohol functional groups, which increases the flexibility of the overall polymer.

Acetic Acid

Acetic Acid or more commonly known as Vinegar when added to the hydrocarbon chain adds a carboxyl group. This functional group will cause the chain to break when affected by a polar molecule. This is the element that will allow for a controlled degradation.

Plastc Facts

Our plastic, along with being an environmentally friendly alternative, is equally if not more effective than other plastics in the market today. Through our testing, we found that our designed plastic has the ability to hold about 2,000 times its own weight which is about the same amount of weight that the average plastic bag can hold. From testing the plastic, it was also found that our plastic can degrade completely in only 30 days. This number is conservative as the water was still and in an ocean environment the plastic would degrade faster.

Formation of the plastic

In order for the plastic to have strength as well as be able to degrade we need to be able to create a long hydrocarbon/polymer chain which exists in a slight thermodynamically unfavorable state (Low Magnitude Positive Gibbs Free Energy). This slight unfavorability will lead to slow controlled degradation after usage. Since the plastic exists in an unfavorable state we will need to apply energy, which in our case will be in the form of heat. In order to reach a state of unnaturally high energy we need to apply energy during the creating. The energy that forms longer polymer chains is known as Thermosetting.

Why Care?

Over a hundred years after the invention of a revolutionary material, we see the demand for plastics at an all time high. While the applications of plastic are very widespread and have a large impact on our daily lives, many people fail to notice the effects of plastics on our environment. People discard plastic products without a second thought, and even those who are environmentally aware only switch to recycling. Our greatest environmental impact isn’t where we walk on land, however, but in fact it on the other 70% of the world. The oceans have been flooded with micro and macro particles of broken down plastics and are reeking havoc on aquatic ecosystems globally. Without anyway to effectively remove these extremely durable, persistent, and malignant objects the situation can only worsen.
